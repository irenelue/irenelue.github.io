Bala Bangles

CONTENT STRATEGY, INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE, WEB DESIGN

For my content strategy course in UCLA's UX Design program, I was given the task of selecting a company and developing a comprehensive redesign strategy to improve its online site. I chose Bala Bangles for my love of health and wellness, and curiosity for the meditation and at-home fitness space. It was a rewarding exercise in which I gained a deeper understanding for applying efficient management strategies in digital platforms, and ensuring content is being effectively delivered to meet user needs and business goals.

WHAT IS BALA BANGLES?

Bala Bangles are one-size-fits-all, wearable weights made for yogis or at-home athletes. They offer versatile tools and weights that can be worn on wrists and ankles for athletic, recreational, and domestic activities such as yoga, aerobics, boxing, pilates, at-home workouts, or core training. It's designed for those that want functional, compact, and beautiful equipment that can take their workouts to the next level and change the way they move.

KICKOFF

Starting off, I asked myself a few initial key questions to gather a deeper understanding of Bala Bangles' customer demographic and any opportunities for improvement with its current online experience.

  1. Who is our user?

    What are their backgrounds, behaviors, intents, and motivations?

  2. What are our users' pain points and goals?

    What are their needs, values, and frustrations?

  3. What are our objectives?

    What challenges will we focus on and address to improve our customers' online experience?

CUSTOMER JOURNEY

To attain a visual understanding of a customer's experience, I created a customer journey map to gain insight into opportunities for improvement within Bala Bangles' site. I divided the customer journey into 5 key stages where each stage encapsulated part of the customer’s interaction with Bala Bangles' online experience. For each stage, I outlined important touch-points and based on all data points, I found that the main area of concern was stage 3, Decision. I found that the best opportunity for improvement was improving the current personalization framework to provide customers personalized content and a tailor-made experience.
