DESIGN CHALLENGE

With the recent COVID-19 pandemic and variants - it has dramatically changed our lives, relationships, and experiences. More specifically, it has changed consumer behavior and businesses have been, and are continuing to adapt - especially for ones which require physical locations. One industry which has been navigating through this challenge are businesses in the fast-food segment.

Our challenge was to create a comprehensive and user-friendly mobile application for a fast-food restaurant, focusing on enhancing the overall meal purchasing experience. While there are several fast-food chains with their own mobile apps, each offering unique experiences, we were specifically tasked with leveraging competitor designs and improving upon them. Our exploration encompassed the entire process, from selecting the order location to preferences for pick-up, as we aimed to identify customer pain points and design solutions that would add value and increase the app's enjoyability.

KICKOFF & PROJECT TIMELINE

Moving forward, we selected Chipotle due to its strong appeal to young millennials and our familiarity with its products and branding. This choice provides us with a solid foundation to dive deeper into the design process and explore innovative solutions.

ASSUMPTIONS MAPPING

We initiated our exploration of potential design directions by creating an assumptions map. It served as a centralized repository for all our assumptions, organized around the following key themes:

  1. Identifying the primary pain points experienced by the customer.
  2. Understanding the desired end result the customer aims to achieve.
  3. Determining the contributing factors of customer satisfaction.
  4. Uncovering the key elements that would encourage repeat usage of our product.

To prioritize our assumptions, we assessed each one based on its level of certainty and significance. We asked ourselves the following guiding questions to determine which assumptions warranted the most attention:

  1. Certainty: Is this assumption a commonly encountered issue?
  2. Uncertainty: Does this assumption represent a novel or uncommon problem?
  3. Important: Is this issue critical to resolve and valuable to customers?
  4. Unimportant: Is this issue commonplace and expected by customers?

VALUE PROPOSITION CANVAS

We created a value proposition canvas to ensure a strong market fit and align our product and feature development with the customer's values and needs. Our value map highlighted the importance of timely and accurate orders, real-time order tracking, and options for modifying orders based on dietary restrictions or allergiesm, and providing a seamless and engaging ordering experience.

MEET THE USERS

CUSTOMER JOURNEY

Our goal is to reimagine Chipotle's mobile ordering experience, making it more efficient, enjoyable, and user-friendly. Our team is specifically focused on enhancing the group ordering process and incentivizing customers to use the app regularly. Through our research, we identified discrepancies in group ordering, which is prevalent in corporate and party settings such as social gatherings, family events, or professional settings. Improving order accuracy and streamlining the app's usability are key priorities to enhance the overall customer experience.

Customer Values:

  1. Order accuracy, quality, and efficiency
  2. Safe and timely deliveries
  3. Minimal obstacles within in-app experience

Customer Frustrations:

  1. No option to split payments for group ordering
  2. No discounts/rewards to incentive and retain customers
  3. Inaccurate estimated time of deliveries
  4. Forced registration to begin ordering process
  5. Checking if every member in the group has added or completed their order request

SUCCESS METRICS

KEY OBJECTIVES

Objective 1: Establish Chipotle as the preferred lunch destination for busy office workers and deliver a seamless ordering experience for both new and returning customers, while adhering to COVID protocols.

  1. Decrease time to complete a group order, considering both tech-savvy and non-tech-savvy users.
  2. Boost customer conversion rates by implementing compelling incentives for employees at affiliate companies (i.e. leveraging exclusive discount codes/coupons and streamlining access through badge scanning)

Objective 2: Increase the number of Chipotle advocates by exceeding expectations in food quality, service, and rewards program.

  1. Achieve a 20% reduction in refund requests resulting from customer complaints within the next 6 months.
  2. Attain a customer satisfaction rating of 9-10 on surveys or receive recommendations from 80% of customers.

IDEATION: OBJECTIVE 1

The Problem: The "group order" feature button is not easily discoverable on the home screen. Users struggle to locate it and often overlook it when starting their ordering process. Consequently, they proceed with their order without utilizing the intended feature tailored for their needs.

Proposed Solutions:

  1. Onboarding Process: Introduce a dedicated screen during onboarding that provides a brief explanation of how the feature works and highlights its key benefits, thereby adding value for the customer.
  2. Home Screen Redesign: Revamp the home screen to make the "group feature" call-to-action (CTA) more prominent and easily noticeable. This ensures that users must actively choose their preferred ordering method, including the group order option.
  3. Cart Pop-up Reminder: Implement a pop-up message in the cart section that reminds users of the group order feature. This notification appears when multiple items are added to the cart, giving users the option to utilize the feature for a more streamlined ordering experience.

IDEATION: OBJECTIVE 2

The Problem: The need to incentivize customers to become loyal and frequent visitors to Chipotle. We aim to create an end-to-end experience that leaves a lasting impression and generates a "wow" factor for our customers.

Proposed Solutions:

  1. Timeliness Compensation: When an order is not prepared on time, we will compensate users by offering a free drink or food item. This gesture acknowledges any inconvenience caused and helps maintain customer satisfaction.
  2. In-App Games: To enhance customer engagement, we will incorporate interactive games within the app. Customers can participate in these games and have the opportunity to win free gifts or special deals, fostering a sense of excitement and enjoyment.
  3. Badge Collection System: We will introduce a badge collection feature where customers can earn badges by completing certain actions or milestones. Accumulating badges will allow customers to unlock rewards such as free food items, creating an incentive for continued engagement with the app and the brand.
